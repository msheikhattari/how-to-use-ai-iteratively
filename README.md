# How to Use AI for Hard Problems

A practical guide based on real conversations between real people and Claude (or any AI). The core lesson is one sentence:

**AI gives you the median answer. Your job is to push past it.**

## Who this is for

Anyone learning to use AI (Claude, GPT, Gemini — same principle) for real problems. Especially useful if you've tried AI before and gotten generic advice that didn't move the needle.

This repo started as one person's case study. It's growing into a community resource — contributions welcome (see below).

## The 30-second version

The same AI tool, used by two people with the same question, will produce wildly different outcomes depending on how each person engages with it.

The difference isn't intelligence or talent. It's method.

Most people accept the first answer. Some people push past it — verify the specifics, ask "what runs in parallel?", reframe constraints as variables, volunteer context the AI didn't ask for, reject "this is impossible" framings, demand executable plans instead of option menus.

When you do this, AI stops being an oracle and becomes a thinking partner. The outcomes get exceptional.

## A note on what this is NOT

This isn't an "AI hype" guide. It isn't a list of clever prompts that produce trick outputs. It isn't a productivity hack.

It's a practical method for getting real work done with AI on real problems — building a business, learning a skill, navigating a career change, solving a hard logistical puzzle. The method is built on the observation that AI's first answer is almost always the median, and exceptional outcomes require deliberate iteration.

## Why this exists

A working professional spent a few hours iterating with Claude on a complex multi-year credential path. Starting estimate: 24-28 months. Through deliberate pushback, the realistic timeline compressed to 10-14 months. Same AI, same person — the difference was method.

He asked: "Save this so other people can learn it." This repo is that. And then it became: "Let it grow. Other people have learned things too." Hence the case studies + contributions.

If you've used AI to solve something hard, [share what you learned](./CONTRIBUTING.md). The resource compounds when more people contribute.
