# Examples — How This Applies

The method is universal. Here are concrete examples of how it applies to specific domains.

## For consultants and freelancers

### Median answer trap

AI will tell you "consulting rates in your field range from $X to $Y per hour" — that's the median market answer. If you accept it, you'll be in the middle of the band.

But you're not necessarily in the middle. Your specific credential mix, niche, network, or skill stack might warrant the top of the band. Or there might be a niche where standard rates don't apply at all because almost no one has your combination.

### The push

Instead of:
> "What should I charge for consulting in my field?"

Try:
> "I have these specific credentials: [PhD + 22 years specific experience + niche specialty + AI fluency]. Don't give me generic rate ranges. What does the top 10% of consultants with this profile charge? Cite specific evidence if you can."

Then verify by finding actual senior consultants with similar profiles + checking LinkedIn ProFinder + asking specifically what differentiates premium pricing in your niche.

### The compression

Standard advice will say "it takes 6-12 months to build a sustainable consulting pipeline." That's median. Push:

> "What's the fastest documented case? What did they do differently?"
>
> "I have these existing relationships: [list]. Which warm channel is highest-leverage?"
>
> "Walk me through a plan to get my first 2-3 clients in 4-6 weeks. Be specific."

### Tactical for outreach

Median AI will draft polite "I hope this finds you well" cold messages. That's noise. Push:

> "Draft a cold outreach that has a 30%+ response rate. What makes the difference between 3% and 30%?"
>
> "Adapt this for [specific person at specific company]. Reference their recent work specifically."
>
> "Pick the strongest version of this. Don't give me 3 variants — give me one to send."

## For builders and creators

### Median answer trap

AI will tell you "build a portfolio in one niche to be focused." That's median advice — true for the average teen builder, but you might not be average.

If you have AI-fluency + range across canvases (Minecraft + 3D + web + design) + builder mindset that older creators don't have, your edge is RANGE. Trade what's uncommon for what's common and you become average.

### The push

Instead of:
> "How should I structure my portfolio?"

Try:
> "I'm 16 with AI-tooling fluency, range across [list canvases], and existing paying client experience. What's the portfolio structure that signals my actual edge — not generic teen-builder advice?"

### The compression

Standard advice says "Minecraft builders take a year to reach $500/month." Median. Push:

> "What's the fastest documented case? What did they do differently?"
>
> "Walk me through a plan to hit $500/month in 4-6 months. Be specific about pricing strategy, platform choice, and weekly time commitment."

### Tactical for pricing

Median Fiverr advice for teen builders: "start at $5-15 to gather reviews." That's a $5/hour effective rate. Push:

> "What's the highest entry pricing for a teen builder with strong portfolio + AI-augmented workflow + multi-canvas range? Not median teen — top 10%."
>
> "Show me a Fiverr gig page that would get accepted at $25-50 entry pricing on the strength of the work, not the seller's age."

## For students planning skill paths

### Median answer trap

AI will tell you "the standard path is degree → internship → entry-level → promotion." That's the median path. It works for the average person.

But you might have a non-standard combination of skills, capacity, or interests that suggests a different path. The standard path optimizes for the average outcome; if you're aiming for an exceptional outcome, the standard path is suboptimal.

### The push

Instead of:
> "How should I prepare for a career in [field]?"

Try:
> "I have these existing skills: [list]. I have these interests: [list]. What's the path that compounds these into something exceptional rather than something average?"

> "What's the path that 95% of people in this field don't take? Why don't they take it? What would the upside be if I did?"

### The compression

Standard timeline says "5-7 years from start to professional credentialing." Push:

> "Walk me through every gated requirement. Mark which are truly fixed vs which have alternative pathways. Find the alternative pathways."
>
> "What parts of this can run in parallel with other parts? Where's the actual binding constraint?"
>
> "Who would I call to verify if these regulations are as strict as written?"

## For people designing a career change

### Median answer trap

AI will give you "transition gradually over 12-18 months" advice. That's median. Some people transition faster; some need to transition slower. The right pace is yours, not median.

### The push

> "Here's my situation: [current career + skills + financial obligations + risk tolerance + actual capacity]. Don't give me generic career-change advice. What's the specific path for me?"
>
> "What are the binding constraints? What looks like a constraint but isn't actually one?"
>
> "Where can my current skills bridge into the new direction faster than starting from scratch?"

### The compression

Standard advice: "build your credentials in the new field, then transition." Median.

> "What's the fusion approach — using my existing skills to enter the new field while I build credentials? Specific organizations or companies where this works."
>
> "Where would my current skills be unusually valuable in the new field? That's where I should start, not from the bottom."

## For someone solving a "this seems impossible" problem

### Median answer trap

AI will tell you "this is the standard approach" or "industry best practice." That's median.

If everyone in your industry is doing the same thing and getting the same results, the standard approach can't produce exceptional results by definition.

### The push

> "What's the standard approach? Now: what would I do if I didn't accept the standard approach? What's the outlier path?"

> "Who has solved a problem like this in an unconventional way? What did they do?"

> "What assumptions in the standard approach might be wrong for my specific situation?"

### The reframe

> "This constraint feels binding. What would have to be true for it not to bind?"
>
> "Is there a different framing of the problem where this constraint doesn't apply?"
>
> "What if I solved an adjacent problem that bypasses this constraint?"

## The pattern across all these examples

Notice what's consistent:

1. **Don't accept median framings.** "Standard advice," "typical timelines," "industry-best-practice" are all median. Push past.
2. **Volunteer your specific context.** The AI doesn't know your skills, capacity, network, or constraints. Tell it.
3. **Ask for the outlier path.** "What's the path 95% don't take? Why?"
4. **Verify with primary sources.** AI hallucinates specifics. Confirm.
5. **Demand executable plans.** Menus are easier for the AI; plans are better for you.
6. **Reject impossibility framings.** Institutional defaults can be negotiated. Constraints can be reframed.

The method works across domains because the underlying observation is universal: **AI gives median answers**. Your job is to push past — in any domain.

## Practice

Pick one domain you care about. Open a Claude conversation. Use 2-3 of the techniques above. See what shifts.

The first time, it might feel awkward. By the third or fourth time, it's natural. After a month, you won't think about it — you'll just do it.

That's how exceptional outcomes get built.
