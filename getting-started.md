# Getting Started — If You're New to AI

If you've used Claude or ChatGPT before, skip this and go to [the-method.md](./the-method.md).

If you haven't, this is a 5-minute introduction.

## The basics

AI assistants like Claude (from Anthropic), ChatGPT (from OpenAI), and Gemini (from Google) are tools you can talk to in plain language. You type a question or describe a problem, and they respond.

You don't need to learn special commands. You don't need to be technical. You just have to talk to them like you'd talk to a knowledgeable colleague who has read a lot.

## What they're good at

- **Drafting** — emails, proposals, scripts, content of all kinds
- **Brainstorming** — generating ideas, options, possibilities
- **Research** — synthesizing information across topics, finding patterns
- **Analysis** — breaking down complex topics into understandable pieces
- **Planning** — building step-by-step approaches to goals
- **Problem-solving** — thinking through obstacles, finding alternatives
- **Learning** — explaining things at your level, answering follow-up questions

## What they're NOT good at

- **Recent events** — they may not know what happened last week
- **Verified specifics** — they hallucinate program names, prices, dates; verify with primary sources
- **Your specific context** — they don't know your life, skills, constraints unless you tell them
- **Making promises about the future** — they can plan, but they can't guarantee outcomes
- **Replacing human judgment** — final decisions are yours

## How to start

### Option 1: Claude.ai (easiest, web-based)

1. Go to claude.ai
2. Sign up (free tier exists; Pro is ~$20/month for more usage)
3. Start a conversation by typing in the box

That's it. You don't need anything else.

### Option 2: Claude Code (more powerful, requires a computer + terminal)

If you're going to use AI for ongoing work (planning, drafting, research), Claude Code lets you have a structured workspace with files Claude reads automatically.

This is what Mo (the case study person) uses. It's more setup but more powerful for sustained work.

To install:
1. Install Node.js (search "install node.js" — the official site has a downloader)
2. Open Terminal (Mac) or Command Prompt (Windows)
3. Run: `npm install -g @anthropic-ai/claude-code`
4. Open a folder where you want to work, then run `claude`

If this is technical territory you've never crossed, start with Claude.ai instead. You can always move to Claude Code later.

## Your first conversation

Pick a real problem you have. Not a test question — something you actually want to solve.

Examples:
- "I want to start a side business but don't know what kind. Help me think through this based on my skills."
- "I'm trying to learn a new skill but feel scattered. Help me design a focused 90-day plan."
- "I'm stuck on this writing project: [paste]. What's weakest about it?"
- "I want to read more books. What 5 should I read this year given my interests in [X, Y, Z]?"
- "Help me plan a trip to [place] that's different from what most tourists do."

Type your question. Read the answer. Then apply the method:

- Don't accept the first answer
- Ask for specifics
- Push for parallelization
- Volunteer your context
- Demand executable plans

Iterate until the answer is actually useful to you.

## The first hour

Spend an hour with one real problem. Iterate aggressively. Apply the patterns from [the-method.md](./the-method.md).

After an hour, you'll have:
- A real plan or draft you can use
- Felt experience of how the method works
- Sense of where AI is strong vs weak for your specific needs

Most importantly, you'll have stopped thinking of AI as "search engine on steroids" and started thinking of it as a thinking partner.

## What to do next

After your first real conversation:

1. Read [the-method.md](./the-method.md) carefully if you haven't yet
2. Read [prompts.md](./prompts.md) and bookmark the ones that fit your work
3. Read [examples.md](./examples.md) for the domain closest to yours
4. Try the method on 2-3 different real problems over the next week

By the end of the week, you'll have internalized the method. After a month, you'll be doing this without thinking about it.

That's how exceptional outcomes get built — not through tricks, but through changing your stance toward the tool.

## A note on privacy + data

When you use Claude, your conversations stay between you and Anthropic (the company that makes Claude). They don't show up publicly. Anthropic uses some conversations to improve the model, but you can opt out of this in settings.

Don't paste anything truly sensitive (passwords, full social security numbers, etc.) into any AI conversation. Use AI for thinking; use other tools for secrets.

## Last thing

You don't need to be technical to use AI well. You need to be willing to iterate.

That's the whole game.
