# The Method — 7 Patterns

The full method. Each pattern is a move you can make in conversation with AI to push past the median answer.

## The principle

**AI gives you the median answer.** Your job is to push past it.

Median = average of what most people would do, average constraints, average optimism. That's not what you're trying to do. You're trying to do something exceptional. Exceptional outcomes require pushing through median framings.

The same AI, used by two people with the same question, produces wildly different outcomes. The difference is method, not intelligence.

## The 7 patterns

### 1. Don't accept the first numbers

When AI says "this takes X months" or "this costs Y dollars" or "you need Z hours" — those are usually conservative defaults, not actual floors.

**Tactic:** ask the AI to walk through the reasoning. Ask for sources. If the number seems too high or low, find a primary source and confirm.

**Example:** AI said "Paramedic program is 1,200-1,800 hours." Person found a state-specific source saying 1,100 and asked: "is that all?" The AI corrected itself. That correction unlocked further compression.

### 2. Push for parallelization

AI loves sequences ("first do A, then B, then C"). Many sequences contain parallel possibilities that the AI hasn't surfaced.

**Tactic:** when AI gives you a sequence, ask: "what can run in parallel?"

**Example:** AI laid out "complete credential → wait → start next phase." Person asked: "can these run together?" The answer was yes for several steps, compressing the timeline by 30-50%.

### 3. Verify everything

AI hallucinates. It will tell you programs are 11 months when they're actually 16-21 months. It will quote prices from years ago. It will list providers that have closed or pivoted.

**Tactic:** trust the framework, verify the specifics. Ask the AI to cite sources. Make calls to actual institutions. Confirm with primary documents.

**Example:** Person verified program details by calling and checking websites. Three different programs that AI initially recommended got eliminated — wrong scope, wrong timing, wrong format.

### 4. Reframe constraints as variables

When AI presents a "requirement," ask if there's an alternative form of the requirement.

**Tactic:** "Is this actually fixed, or is there an alternative form of the constraint?"

**Example:** AI said "you need 12 months of prior field experience." Person asked: "what about the 150-call alternative?" That alternative was feasible in 4-5 months of intensive work, not 12 months elapsed. Completely changed the plan.

### 5. Add capacity context the AI didn't ask for

AI doesn't know your specific life. It defaults to assuming standard 40-hour work weeks, average available time, median financial constraints. None of this is necessarily true for you.

**Tactic:** volunteer the relevant constraints AND capacity. Tell the AI when you have extra time blocks. Tell it about flexible income periods. Tell it what specific skills you already have.

**Example:** Person volunteered "I have about half the year as flexible time at work." That changed the entire plan — intensive blocks became viable; the compressed timeline became real.

### 6. Reject "this is impossible" framings

AI is reading what's written down. Institutions are run by humans. Humans can be talked to.

**Tactic:** when AI says "this is the policy" or "this can't be done faster" — ask if you can call and negotiate.

**Example:** AI said "the program has monthly skills lab cadence; that's the binding constraint." Person responded: "I'll call them and negotiate." That single move could compress the plan by 3-4 months — IF the program is flexible. The AI can't make the call; you can.

### 7. Build executable plans, not option menus

AI loves giving you "five options to consider." That's because it doesn't want to be wrong. But you don't need options; you need a plan.

**Tactic:** if AI gives you a menu, ask: "what would you actually do? Walk me through the steps."

**Example:** After several rounds of option menus, person asked: "Build me a phase-by-phase plan with checkpoints." The AI delivered a wave-based execution plan with concrete checkable items per phase. The plan was actionable; the menus weren't.

## The meta-rule

**AI is a thinking partner, not an oracle.**

When you treat it as an oracle, you get the median answer.

When you treat it as a thinking partner — pushing, verifying, reframing, adding context — you get exceptional outcomes.

The difference between average and exceptional often isn't intelligence or talent. It's the willingness to push past the first answer.

## When to apply

This method works best on:
- Multi-month or multi-year planning (career path, credentialing, business strategy)
- Complex problems with multiple constraints
- Situations where the "standard advice" doesn't fit your specific situation
- Anywhere you need a real plan, not generic advice

It's overkill for simple questions ("what's the capital of France"). It's essential for hard ones.

## When NOT to apply

If you're learning a new domain entirely, take the AI's first answer as a starting framework. Push back only after you have enough domain knowledge to know what's median vs exceptional.

The method assumes you have some judgment about what's possible. Build that judgment first if you don't have it yet — then apply the method.

## A reminder

You don't have to use all 7 patterns every conversation. Pick the ones that fit. The method is the underlying stance — engage AI as a thinking partner, not an oracle — and the patterns are tactical moves toward that stance.

Practice. Each iteration gets sharper.
